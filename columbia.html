Columbia

Art, Design, Cross Stitch, Typography

Illuminating the disparity women face in the U.S. compared to the rest of the world using cross stitch.

Columbia is a series of seven cross stitch embroidered statements about how being a woman in the United States compares to the rest of the world. Each embroidery is mounted in found gold painted frames.

Source: NPR

Source: Inter-Parliamentary Union

Source: New York Times

Cross stitch is a traditional medium typically used by women in home decor. If the piece includes typography it tends to include generic statements such as "Bless this Home", or Bible verses. I sought out to reclaim the medium in a way that communicates surprising information about what the experience of being a woman in the U.S.

My decision to mount the work in fake gold frames is to emphasize the lies we are told about America. On the outside America seems like a place of hope, progress, and inspiration, but it reality the experience of living here pales in comparison to other developed countries around the world. America has failed so many populations, but especially women.

Source: Newsweek

Source: World Economic Forum

The name Columbia takes inspiration from the female national personification of the United States of the same name. I chose the name for this body of work because it is a more relevant representation of the United States as it pertains to being a woman.
